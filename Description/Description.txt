Customer Shopping Behavior : Summary

The data reflects a total revenue of approximately $233,081, with clothing and male shoppers driving the majority of the sales.

1. Demographic Insights: Gender vs. Spending
The most striking finding is the significant gap in spending between genders.
Male shoppers contributed $157,890, accounting for roughly 68% of total revenue.
Female shoppers contributed $75,191.
Takeaway: The current product mix or marketing strategy is resonating much more strongly with male consumers.

2. Product Performance: Sales Analysis
Revenue is heavily concentrated in specific categories:
Clothing is the clear leader, generating $104,264.
Accessories follow as a strong second at $74,200.
Footwear ($36,093) and Outerwear ($18,524) represent the smaller portions of the business.
Takeaway: Core apparel and accessories are the primary revenue drivers.

3. Marketing Impact: Discounts
Interestingly, the majority of revenue came from full-price purchases rather than discounted ones.
No Discount: $133,670
Discount Applied: $99,411
Takeaway: The brand maintains strong value perception, as customers are willing to pay full price for items. However, discounts still contribute to a significant portion (approx. 43%) of the volume.

4. Seasonal Trends
Sales remain remarkably consistent throughout the year, with only slight fluctuations:
Peak Season: Fall ($60,018)
Lowest Season: Summer ($55,777)
Takeaway: The business does not suffer from extreme seasonality, though there is a slight "uptick" as the year progresses into Fall and Winter.
